Why do Astronmers Need to Use Many Types of Telescopes?

Author: Nicholas Felarca
Published: 6:07 PM Sunday October 13, 2024

Astronomers make use of many telescopes for a variety of reasons. The most important reason for this is that telescopes can only see 1 or 2 parts of the electromagnetic (EM) spectrum at most. From highest energy to lowest energy rays it goes gamma, X-ray, ultraviolet (UV), visible infrared (IR), microwave, and radio. For example, James Webb Space Telescope records in the visible and infrared spectrum. Being able to see in the infrared spectrum is very useful as it helps observe very distant galaxies, which give us more information into the formation of the universe. We see farther back in time the farther we look away because it takes light time to get to us from that point so viewing something 10 billion light years away means that we are seeing something that happened 10 billion years ago. Chandara is an X-Ray telescope, which is better for recording higher energy events as they are at a higher frequency than all other bands besides gamma. This is extremely useful for observing high-mass end of life stars, black holes, and galaxy clusters.
